The Rise of AI in Poker: A Brief History

Poker AI research dates back decades, but the real breakthroughs began in 2017 when Carnegie Mellon's Libratus defeated top professional poker players in heads-up no-limit Texas Hold'em. This was followed by Pluribus in 2019, which conquered six-player tables, proving that AI could handle the complexity of multi-player games with imperfect information.

Since then, the technology has trickled down from academic research labs into consumer-facing tools. Today, thousands of players use AI-powered solvers, training platforms, and hand analysis tools as part of their daily study routines.

Key Milestones

  • 2017: Libratus beats top pros in heads-up NLHE over 120,000 hands
  • 2019: Pluribus achieves superhuman play at six-max tables
  • 2020-2022: GTO solvers become mainstream training tools
  • 2023-2024: Real-time AI coaching apps emerge for live play
  • 2025-2026: AI-integrated platforms offer adaptive strategy recommendations

How AI Thinks About Poker

To use AI poker tools effectively, you need to understand how these systems approach the game. Unlike humans, who rely on intuition, reads, and emotional processing, AI poker engines use mathematical frameworks rooted in game theory.

Game Theory Optimal (GTO) Play

GTO strategy aims to be unexploitable. An AI playing GTO does not try to exploit its opponents. Instead, it plays a balanced, mathematically sound strategy that cannot be beaten in the long run, regardless of what the opponent does. Think of it as the defensive baseline: if you play GTO perfectly, no one can have an edge over you.

Exploitative Play

More advanced AI systems go beyond GTO. They observe opponent patterns, bet sizing tendencies, fold frequencies, and timing tells, then deviate from GTO to exploit specific weaknesses. This is where the real money is made, because most human players are far from GTO and have exploitable leaks.

Pro Tip: The best approach in 2026 combines both styles. Use GTO as your foundation, then layer exploitative adjustments based on opponent data. AI tools make this dual approach accessible to everyone.

Essential AI Poker Tools in 2026

The market for AI poker tools has exploded. Here are the categories and what to look for in each.

1. GTO Solvers

Solvers like PioSolver, GTO+, and MonkerSolver compute game-theory-optimal strategies for specific situations. You input a hand scenario, the stack sizes, bet sizes, and ranges, and the solver calculates the mathematically optimal play for both sides.

  • Best for studying specific spots and building your baseline strategy
  • Requires significant computing power for complex multi-street calculations
  • Cloud-based solvers have made this more accessible in 2026

2. Hand History Analyzers

Tools that review your played hands and flag deviations from optimal play. They show you exactly where you are leaving money on the table, whether it is calling too wide on the river, not three-betting enough from the button, or sizing your continuation bets incorrectly.

3. Real-Time Coaching Overlays

The most controversial category. These tools provide suggestions during live play, overlaying recommended actions on your poker client. While powerful, many online poker sites have banned their use during play. Always check your platform's terms of service.

4. Population Analysis Tools

These aggregate data from thousands of players to identify common tendencies at specific stakes. For example, you might learn that players at $0.50/$1.00 online overfold to triple-barrel bluffs by 12% compared to GTO, giving you a clear exploitative adjustment.

Practical AI-Powered Strategies You Can Use Today

Preflop Range Construction

AI has settled many preflop debates. We now know the mathematically correct opening ranges from every position, the optimal three-bet and four-bet frequencies, and how ranges should shift based on stack depth. Study AI-generated preflop charts and memorize them. This alone will give you a significant edge at low and mid-stakes games.

Bet Sizing Optimization

One of the biggest insights from AI poker research is that bet sizing matters far more than most players realize. GTO solvers have revealed that using multiple bet sizes (small, medium, and large) on different board textures is critical. For example:

  • Dry boards (K-7-2 rainbow): Prefer smaller continuation bets (25-33% pot) with a high frequency
  • Wet boards (J-T-8 two-tone): Use larger sizes (66-75% pot) with a more selective range
  • Paired boards: Often favor overbets with polarized ranges

Bluffing Frequency

AI has shown that most human players do not bluff enough. GTO strategy often calls for bluffing at a higher frequency than feels comfortable, especially on later streets. The key is selecting the right bluff candidates: hands with blockers to your opponent's continuing range and little showdown value.

Fold Equity Calculations

Modern AI tools can calculate your fold equity in real time, telling you the exact break-even percentage needed for a bluff to be profitable. Understanding these numbers transforms bluffing from a guessing game into a calculated decision.

Common Mistakes When Using AI Poker Tools

AI tools are powerful, but they can also lead you astray if misused. Here are the pitfalls to avoid.

1. Over-Relying on GTO

Playing pure GTO at low stakes is like bringing a scalpel to a fistfight. Your opponents are making massive errors, and GTO does not punish those errors maximally. Use GTO as your foundation, but always look for exploitative adjustments.

2. Ignoring Practical Factors

AI does not account for tilt, fatigue, table dynamics, or the psychological pressure of playing for meaningful money. These human factors remain critical and cannot be solved by any algorithm.

3. Studying Without Applying

Spending hours in a solver is useless if you cannot execute the strategies at the table. Focus on one concept at a time, drill it until it becomes automatic, then move on.

The goal is not to play like an AI. The goal is to use AI insights to become a better human player.

The Ethics of AI in Poker

The use of AI in poker raises important ethical questions. Where is the line between study tool and unfair advantage? Most of the poker community agrees on a few principles:

  • Studying with AI is fair game. Using solvers to improve your game away from the table is universally accepted.
  • Real-time AI assistance is cheating. Using bots or real-time solvers during play violates the rules of every major poker site.
  • Sharing solver outputs in forums is fine. The poker education community thrives on sharing and discussing AI-derived strategies.
  • Using AI to build ranges and study plans is smart. This is the modern equivalent of reading poker books, just faster and more accurate.

The Future of AI and Poker

Looking ahead, several trends are shaping the future of AI in poker:

  • Personalized AI coaches that learn your specific weaknesses and create custom training programs
  • Voice-activated strategy assistants for reviewing hands and discussing lines between sessions
  • Advanced opponent modeling that builds detailed profiles from limited hand samples
  • AI-powered poker content that generates hand breakdowns, quizzes, and study materials on demand
  • Provably fair AI poker platforms where the dealing algorithm itself is verifiable and transparent
Stay Ahead: The players who embrace AI tools and integrate them into disciplined study routines will have an enormous edge in 2026 and beyond. The gap between AI-assisted players and those who rely purely on intuition will only widen.

Getting Started: Your AI Poker Study Plan

If you are new to AI poker tools, here is a step-by-step plan to get started:

  1. Learn preflop ranges: Download AI-generated preflop charts and study them daily for two weeks
  2. Choose a solver: Start with a free or low-cost option like GTO+ to learn the basics
  3. Review your hands: After every session, analyze your five biggest pots in the solver
  4. Focus on one street at a time: Master flop play before moving to turn and river decisions
  5. Join a study group: Discuss solver outputs with other players to deepen understanding
  6. Track your progress: Keep a database and monitor your win rate over time
