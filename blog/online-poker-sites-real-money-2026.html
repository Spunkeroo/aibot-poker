Best Online Poker Sites for Real Money in 2026: Tested and Ranked

By aibot.poker | March 1, 2026 | 8 min read

Online Poker Is Legal in More States Than Ever

As of 2026, real-money online poker is legal in 9 US states: New Jersey, Pennsylvania, Michigan, Nevada, Delaware, West Virginia, Connecticut, and most recently New York and Illinois. Combined liquidity pools mean bigger tournaments and more action. Here's where to play based on extensive testing.

1. PokerStars — Best Overall

Still the king. Highest traffic worldwide, the most tournament variety (from $1 buy-ins to $25K high rollers), and the best software. The Sunday Million ($1M guaranteed) runs every week. Cash games run 24/7 at every stake from $0.01/0.02 to $200/400. Welcome bonus: 100% deposit match up to $600 released as you play. Available in NJ, PA, MI. The gold standard for a reason.

2. WSOP.com — Best for US Players

The World Series of Poker's official site. Available in NJ, PA, MI, NV with shared player pools. The big draw: online satellites to live WSOP events in Las Vegas. Win your $10,000 Main Event seat for as little as $1. Software has improved massively since 2024. Freeroll tournaments daily — you can literally start playing for free and build a bankroll.

3. BetMGM Poker — Best Bonuses

BetMGM offers the most generous new player package: $75 free play + 100% deposit match up to $1,000. Part of the partypoker US network (shared player pool with partypoker and Borgata Poker). Software is clean and mobile-friendly. Especially good for casual players — the player pool is softer than PokerStars. Available in NJ, PA, MI.

What to Look For (and Avoid)

Look for: State licensing (confirms legality and player protection), fast withdrawals (under 3 business days), responsive customer support, and active player pools at your preferred stakes. Avoid: Offshore/unregulated sites — they're illegal in the US, offer no player protections, and can freeze your funds. If a site isn't licensed by a US state gaming commission, don't deposit. Also avoid sites with mostly bot traffic (check player-to-table ratios).

Bankroll Management for New Players

The #1 reason players go broke: playing stakes they can't afford. Cash games: have at least 20 buy-ins for your stake ($200 for $0.05/0.10). Tournaments: 50 buy-ins minimum ($500 for $10 tournaments). Start at the lowest stakes — $0.01/0.02 cash or $1-3 tournaments. The goal isn't to play high; it's to beat your current level consistently before moving up. Track your results, study regularly, and move up when your bankroll allows.
